# Mamut2026 TDVRPTW benchmark family (OSM road-network time-dependent, 10-1000 customers)

Satellite benchmark repository of [MAMUT-routing](https://github.com/ANR-MAMUT/MAMUT-routing) (ANR MAMUT, ANR-22-CE22-0016), mounted there as `benchmarks/TDVRPTW/Mamut2026`. Self-contained: instances and best-known solutions ship together; the time-dependent travel times are stored as a compact **road-graph** specification and materialize deterministically on load — no committed arrival-time-function sidecars.

## What this family is

Workbench-generated time-dependent instances whose congestion is **derived from real city road networks** (OpenStreetMap), deliberately *not* based on the classic IGP 2003 category-times-profile construction (for an IGP-based large-scale family see `Lera2026` in MAMUT-routing). Customers and depot are sampled on the road graph of a real city (POI-based or hybrid sampling); per-edge hourly speed profiles over a 24 h day (horizon `[0, 86400]` seconds) come from one of two synthetic traffic models; per-arc travel times are the exact result of driving the fastest free-flow path through those time-varying speeds. Rush-hour congestion therefore emerges from the network structure: arterial corridors slow down at peak, side streets mostly keep free flow, and detours can beat congested corridors depending on the departure time.

Two traffic models, both shipped (the model tag is part of every instance name):

- **bpr** — synthetic commuter simulation: a commuter population (homes uniform on the network, workplaces weighted toward amenity POIs) fires morning/lunch/evening trips with normal departure-time mixtures; trips are routed on free-flow fastest paths; per-edge hourly flows are accumulated at edge entry times and converted to speeds by the Bureau of Public Roads volume-delay function `t = t_free (1 + 0.15 (flow/capacity)^4)` with road-class capacities (multiplier capped at 6).
- **wave** — no simulation: each edge gets a bimodal rush-hour speed dip (peaks around 08:15 and 17:45) scaled by road class, distance to the city center, and a seeded per-edge jitter.

Three intensities per model (light / moderate / heavy) scale the commuter count (bpr) or the dip amplitude (wave). Cities: Lyon, Paris, San-Francisco, Hong-Kong, Tokyo (central extracts). Sizes n in {10, 25, 50, 100, 500, 1000} customers; customer sampling methods poi and hyb (hybrid POI + parametric).

Each customer carries a hard time window `[e_i, l_i]` (service may start in the window; arrival before `e_i` waits; arrival after `l_i` is infeasible), synthesized by a time-dependent route-centered method: windows are centered on visit times simulated along a TD nearest-neighbour tour and repaired to TD feasibility bounds, so every customer is individually serveable within the horizon.

## Format (`road-graph` td model)

- `<name>.vrp.json` — MAMUT TD shape: depot 0, coordinates (local ENU meters), demands, service times, time windows, `horizon [0, 86400]`, and a `td` block `{model: "road-graph", graph_path, graph_sha256, atf_sha256}`.
- `<name>.road.json.gz` — road-graph sidecar (`format: "mamut-td-road-graph"`): the trimmed road subgraph the instance lives on (the union of the pinned fastest paths between all instance nodes) as directed edges `[u, v, length_m, speeds[24]]` with strictly positive per-hour speeds (FIFO by construction), vertex OSM node ids, the instance-node-to-vertex map, and the materialization parameters (`bin_edges`, `sample_step`, `simplify_tolerance`, `extension_end`). Hashed over its uncompressed canonical bytes (`graph_sha256`).
- **No ATF sidecar**: the canonical arrival-time functions (one non-decreasing continuous piecewise-linear function per arc of the complete graph) are **materialized on load** by `mamut_routing_lib.td` (>= 0.5.0): pinned deterministic Dijkstra over free-flow times fixes one canonical fastest path per arc; exact per-edge arrival functions are sampled exactly along that path on the fixed departure grid (pointwise error cannot accumulate); the samples are decimated deterministically (exact-collinear drop, then Douglas-Peucker with the sidecar tolerance). `atf_sha256` pins the materialized canonical bytes exactly as a committed sidecar's hash would — integrity is verifiable offline from published data alone. Note: n=1000 materialization takes minutes and several GB of RAM; solvers should materialize once and reuse.

Naming: `mamut-<city>-n<N>-<model>-<intensity>-<method>` under `fastest/<city>/n=<N>/<name>/` (travel derives from free-flow-fastest paths). Best-known solutions ship as `<name>.bks.Duration.json` sidecars (objective: Duration minimization — the depot departure time of each route is a decision variable; costs are the authoritative output of the canonical checker `mamut_routing_lib.td.check_td_solution`).

## Reproducibility

Instances are generated by the MAMUT-routing workbench stage 3 (`webapp/td_traffic.jl` + `mamut-routing-publish workbench`); generation parameters, traffic seeds and provenance are recorded in each instance's `metadata` and in the sidecar `generator` block. Materialized travel times are reproducible from published data alone via the pinned lib code path.
